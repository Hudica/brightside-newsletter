Positivity Score

How is the score calculated?

Gathering the Content

The process begins with collecting headlines from various news sources. Using RSS feeds, we access headlines from a wide range of reputable news organizations. This diverse selection helps ensure a balanced representation of sentiments expressed in the media. Typically, this process yields approximately 900 headlines, encompassing multiple genres to mitigate positivity bias.

Generating a Score

Once the headlines are compiled, each one is individually analyzed by our sentiment analysis model. The model categorizes each headline into one of three sentiments: Positive, Neutral, or Negative. To compute the overall positivity score, we calculate the ratio of positive headlines to the total number of headlines, resulting in a percentage that reflects the proportion of positive sentiments.

Summary

The positivity score is calculated as follows:
Score = (Number of Positive Headlines / Total Number of Headlines) Ã— 100
It is the % of positive headlines out of all headlines.
